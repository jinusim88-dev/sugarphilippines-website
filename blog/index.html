Raw vs. Refined vs. Washed: Which Sugar Does Your Business Need?

March 10, 2026 · 4 min read

If you're buying sugar in bulk, you've probably come across different labels — raw, refined, and washed. But what do these actually mean for your products, your costs, and your operations?

Here's a practical breakdown to help you choose the right type for your business.

Refined Sugar

Refined sugar goes through the most processing. It's white, fine-grained, and dissolves quickly. This makes it ideal for applications where clarity and consistency matter — think beverages, confectionery, and white bread.

Best for: Bakeries making pastries, cakes, and white bread. Beverage manufacturers. Any product where the sugar needs to dissolve cleanly without adding color.

Brands we carry: Busco Premium, Mitrphol Refined Sugar.

Raw Sugar

Raw sugar retains some of the natural molasses from the cane. It has a golden-brown color, slightly larger crystals, and a mild caramel flavor. It costs less than refined sugar and works well when color isn't a concern.

Best for: Food manufacturers, sauces, kakanin, and products where a bit of color and natural flavor is acceptable. Also popular as a feedstock for re-processing.

Brands we carry: Biscom Raw.

Washed Sugar

Washed sugar sits between raw and refined. The outer molasses layer is washed off, but it doesn't go through full refining. The result is a lighter color than raw, at a lower cost than refined.

Best for: Commercial kitchens, bakeries producing pan de sal or ensaymada, and operations that want a balance of quality and budget.

Brands we carry: Passi Washed Sugar.

Quick Comparison

Here's how they stack up:

  • Color: Refined (white) → Washed (off-white) → Raw (golden brown)
  • Price: Raw (lowest) → Washed (mid) → Refined (highest)
  • Dissolving speed: Refined (fastest) → Washed → Raw (slowest)
  • Best use: Depends on your product's requirements for color, taste, and texture
